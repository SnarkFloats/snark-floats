The Rules

I gave myself just three rules when I started this project. Okay, that's a lie. I started with more. I gave myself three that I stuck with!

  1. No casting anyone as a character they voiced in the game. (Sorry Seth Green. ü•π)
  2. Age doesn't matter too much if it's a role where the face and/or body can't be seen, but if it's a role that requires face- or body-time on camera, they have to be able to pull it off.
  3. They must be alive. (Yeah, I know...)

The other rules I ditched were ultimately too limiting. For example, you'll note my choices tend to sometimes be a litle older than the character was originally intended to be. I thought about that rule a long time before I ditched it. Let's face it, none of them are really playing their age (given the 105 year old Asari and the likely 1000+ year old Krogan).

In the end, I chose actors who felt RIGHT to me. Who felt like they fit the character not just in a surface way, but like they could really portray who they are.

It matters. Because one of the best things about ME is how layered and complex so many of the characters really are. I wanted to honor that.

I broke the whole thing into four sections.

  • Main Characters: these are people we got to know well as companions or recurring enemies, often in more than one game
  • ME1 Supporting Cast: characters who appeared first or mainly in the first game but aren't really main characters
  • ME2 Supporting Cast: showed up first or mainly in the second game but aren't really main characters
  • ME3 Supporting Cast: you guessed it... they showed up in the third game but aren't really main characters
